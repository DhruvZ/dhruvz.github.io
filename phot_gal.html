Reimagining SED Fitting with Cosmological Simulations and Machine Learning

My second project as a graduate student was to construct an SED fitting tool based on simulations. SED fitting is the process by which observers derive the properties of galaxies from observations; in general, it takes the form of building up a toy model of a galaxy until the model matches the input data. However, this process collapses a 3-dimensional galaxy into a single point and applies many assumptions which can bias inferred galaxy properties.

However, in theory being able to use a simulated galaxy would remove some of these assumptions. I constructed a simulation-based-inference (SBI) pipeline Phot-Gal trained off of a wide variety of simulations and synthetic observations. These simulations came from the CAMELS-1P suite of cosmological volumes with the SIMBA and IllustrisTNG models where the galaxy physics parameters were varied one at a time. With this, I was intending to make it so that Phot-Gal was not tied to the assumptions of any particular model or the single set of parameters associated with the fiducial galaxy models. Phot-Gal is a first-of-its-kind SBI tool that recovers galaxy properties trained off of simulations that can process inputs with any combination of photometry, works over many redshifts, and returns robust uncertainties. Phot-Gal was successful in recovering the properties of the test galaxies and did better than traditional observational tools, but it did not always have appropriate uncertainties (though usually they were still better than the observational predictions). I analyzed the details of when and why Phot-Gal was failing and associated it with galaxies that are outliers in the stellar mass and stellar age plane. Phot-Gal is downloadable!
