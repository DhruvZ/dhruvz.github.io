Attenuation Curves in the very early Universe

JWST is giving astronomers a first peak at the very early Universe that we’ve never had before. However, it has been spotting very bright galaxies that SED fitting is inferring to be very massive. A big question with fitting the galaxies JWST has seen is whether the assumptions about the dust laws people tend to use for nearby galaxies hold up well. Dust at high-z is likely different for several reasons: the primary formation mechanism, the presence of heavy elements, and the lack of time for collisional processes to significantly change the dust properties. McKinney et a. 2025 showed that many observed galaxies have a preference for ‘flatter’ dust attenuation laws following this argument.

I am using cosmological galaxy zoom-in simulations run with the SMUGGLE physics to answer the question of how we should expect dust to theoretically impact the spectrum of high-z galaxies. The SMUGGLE model is uniquely able to tackle an inquiry into early dust because it models many different sizes of dust grains and how they evolve with time. Our radiative transfer then forward models this with spatially varying dust extinction laws. Our results are currently being written up, expect them on the arxiv soon!
